FOOD BANK DESIGN PROJECT

REIMAGINING THE FOOD BANK AS A SPACE FOR SHARING, GATHERING, AND GROWING.

This project transforms the traditional concept of a food bank into a space for sharing food, fostering community through a community garden, shared eating spaces, and an open marketplace.

The design process began with the creation of sketch models to explore and refine a continuous form that fosters seamless transitions between spaces. The overarching goal was to create an inviting and inclusive environment where people could come together, exchange food, and build community connections. This vision informed the conceptual framework of the structure as a transitional space that merges function and flow.

The chosen site is a park frequently used as a pedestrian thoroughfare, making it an ideal location for this communal food bank The building’s placement and orientation were carefully considered to complement the site’s natural pathways and enhance its role as a community hub.

WEST ELEVATION

The structure is a continuous steel form, creating a cohesive experience that bridges the urban and natural aspects of the site. The food bank incorporates a cascading garden, a marketplace, and shared spaces for communal dining. The open design encourages interaction and inclusivity, while the integration of green spaces promotes sustainability and community involvement.
