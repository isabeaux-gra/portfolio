TO COMPRESS: A PAVILION DESIGN

AN ARCHITECTURAL EXPLORATION OF COMPRESSION, TENSION, AND THE SHIFTING OF FAULT LINES

This project began with a single verb: to compress. Inspired by accordion folds, the building organizes space into tight, rhythmic layers that visually convey compresion. As one moves through the structure, the walls press in from above and on either side, creating a physical sense of compression.

Strategic cuts, resembling geological fault lines, slice through the folded mass, allowing movement and light to penetrate. These incisions create pathways that invite flow across, upward, and downward, transforming the dense structure to allow transition and movement.

TOURISM in the SCOTTISH HIGHLANDS

CHRONICLING THE IMPACT OF THE TOURISM INDUSTRY IN THE HIGHLANDS OF SCOTLAND

This story map examines the role of tourism in shaping the economy of the Scottish Highlands, a region renowned for its breathtaking landscapes and cultural heritage. The project traces tourism’s historical rise, from its roots in Queen Victoria’s era to its modern-day prominence as a vital driver of Scotland’s economy.

Key topics include employment in tourism, revenue patterns, and visitor distribution and patterns. The analysis highlights the opportunities and challenges posed by tourism growth, emphasizing the need for sustainable development to balance economic benefits with environmental preservation and local community well-being.
